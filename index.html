Abstract

Background: Soldiers deployed in remote or hostile environments face life-threatening health risks with no real-time monitoring capability. Existing systems fail to provide continuous, automated, and integrated health and position data over battlefield distances.

Proposed System: This paper presents an IoT-based Soldier Health Monitoring and Position Tracking System using the LoRaWAN protocol. The wearable transmitter unit — built around the ESP32 microcontroller — integrates the MAX30102 heart rate sensor, DHT11 temperature and humidity sensor, and NEO-6M GPS module. Data packets are wirelessly transmitted via an SX1278 LoRa module at 433 MHz to an Arduino Nano–based receiver at the base station. A real-time web dashboard built with Node.js and Socket.io displays all parameters with SAFE/DANGER alerts.

Results: The system achieved stable heart rate readings (91–94 BPM), body temperature (34.0–35.3°C), and reliable GPS coordinates with end-to-end latency of approximately 5 seconds. No packet loss was observed during indoor testing. The danger alert threshold logic operated correctly, distinguishing abnormal heart rate (>100 BPM) from temperature conditions (>37.5°C).

Conclusion: The proposed system demonstrates that low-cost IoT components combined with LoRaWAN communication can deliver a reliable, low-latency soldier health monitoring platform suitable for real-world battlefield deployment.

§ 01

Introduction

Modern warfare demands real-time situational awareness — not only of enemy positions but also of the physiological health of allied soldiers. Troops deployed in remote, hostile, or geographically challenging environments are routinely exposed to extreme physical stress that can rapidly deteriorate vital parameters. Traditional communication and monitoring methods have proven inadequate in delivering timely, accurate, and comprehensive health data from the battlefield.

The rapid advancement of Internet of Things (IoT) technologies, wireless sensor networks, and low-power wide-area communication protocols has made it feasible to design compact, wearable, and highly effective health monitoring systems that can operate over long distances with minimal power consumption. LoRaWAN (Long Range Wide Area Network) technology has emerged as a leading communication standard for such applications, offering multi-kilometer range with very low energy usage — ideal for deployments where cellular coverage is unavailable.

"The absence of a unified, real-time, integrated health and position tracking platform represents a critical gap in existing battlefield monitoring systems — one that IoT-based LoRaWAN solutions can directly address."

This work proposes an integrated Soldier Health Monitoring and Position Tracking System that continuously monitors heart rate and body temperature alongside real-time GPS-based position, and transmits this data wirelessly to a central monitoring station using LoRaWAN over the SX1278 RF module at 433 MHz.

§ 07

Conclusion

This paper presented an integrated Soldier Health Monitoring and Position Tracking System using LoRaWAN technology. The system successfully demonstrated real-time acquisition of heart rate, body temperature, and GPS coordinates, with reliable long-range wireless transmission using LoRa at 433 MHz. The web-based dashboard provided live visualization, instant SAFE/DANGER alerts, device disconnect detection, and interactive GPS map tracking.

The proposed system directly addresses the critical gap in existing battlefield monitoring infrastructure — the absence of a unified, real-time, integrated health and position tracking platform that can operate in environments without cellular coverage. With zero packet loss in indoor testing and ~5 second end-to-end latency, the system is well-suited for deployment in defense and remote surveillance applications.

Future enhancements include additional biomedical sensors (SpO2, ECG, blood pressure), AI-based predictive health analytics, multi-soldier platoon-wide LoRaWAN network support, cloud integration, and hardware miniaturization with custom PCB design.
